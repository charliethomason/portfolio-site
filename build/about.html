About Charlie Thomason

My full name is Charles Lee Thomason, Jr. and I’m a Front-end Web Developer and Artist living in Chicago, Illinois. I’ve also worked as a Web & Graphic Designer, Internet Marketing Specialist, Journalist, and Video Editor. I was born in Red Bank, New Jersey in 1984, went to high school & college in Kentucky, and moved to Chicago in 2008.

My specialties as a Web Developer include web accessibility, responsive design, cross-browser compatibility, and rapid prototyping. I am currently employed full-time as a Presentation Layer Engineer for Razorfish, where I’ve been happily coding since January 2013. I studied web development at The Starter League (then known as Code Academy) in early 2012, and received my Masters in New Media Studies from DePaul University in 2010.

I’ve always considered myself a visually creative person, and earned my Bachelor of Fine Arts in Art Studio from the University of Kentucky in 2007. I’ve had paintings, drawings, and photographs featured in exhibits in Lexington, KY and Chicago—including an exhibit dedicated to Chicago’s Bloomingdale Trail. In 2012, I self-published my first photography book, Open Range: From Monument Valley to the Mojave Desert. I am currently working on my second book, which will feature paintings of birds found in the Chicagoland area.

I currently live in the South Loop neighborhood of Chicago with my fiancé, Amanda, and our 2 cats. I love movies, auto racing, LEGO, and playing guitar, bass, and piano. I am a progressive Catholic, registered Democrat, and member at Old St. Pat’s Church. I am a happy, peaceful, open-minded person and love traveling and meeting new people.
